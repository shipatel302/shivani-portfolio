About

Turning complex data
into actionable insight

I'm a data scientist and bioinformatician with 4+ years of experience applying machine learning, predictive modeling, and advanced statistical analysis to large-scale genomic and clinical datasets.

At the University of Delaware, I analyze HPV-positive cancer cohorts from TCGA, build Random Forest classifiers for tumor subtype prediction (AUC 0.85), and optimize scalable NGS pipelines on AWS and SLURM HPC. I also manage IRB-compliant REDCap and EHR databases for neurodegenerative disease research.

Before that, two years at GSK (via EduRamp) leading multi-omics integration across oncology and retinal dystrophy cohorts, working with scRNA-seq, bulk RNA-seq, WGS/WES, LFQ proteomics, and clinical data, building workflows that directly informed therapeutic prioritization strategy.

I build things that are reproducible, deployable, and scientifically defensible. Every pipeline and model is aimed at making complex data actionable across multiple domains.
